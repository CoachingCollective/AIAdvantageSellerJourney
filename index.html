Listing Presentation
The agent wins or loses the listing here
▾
Objective
Present a personalized, differentiated strategy that earns the seller's trust and the signed listing agreement.
What the Presentation Must Cover
  • The agent's personal brand and track record
  • Brokerage reach — global network, syndication, brand recognition
  • Specific marketing strategy for this property — digital, print, social, brokerage network
  • Pricing strategy — data-driven, grounded in the market
  • The agent's differentiation — what they will do for this seller that others won't

Presentation Delivery

In-person is strongly preferred for luxury. The agent presents the listing presentation digitally — screen-shared, on a tablet, or projected — and leaves a professionally printed, bound version with the seller. The print version is not optional. It is what the seller references after the agent leaves the room.

Digital version — PDF, flipbook, or screen-shareable format — is also available for Zoom situations or as a follow-up leave-behind.

Print quality matters. The presentation is produced at a luxury standard. It reflects the level of service being promised.

Contract Signed + Admin Setup
Nothing gets executed — everything gets started
▾
Objective
The moment the contract is signed, think through the entire pre-launch sequence and set everything in motion. Every item gets an owner, a timeline, and a plan.
⚑ Critical First Step — Before Anything Goes to the Seller
The listing agent does an independent solo walkthrough of the property — without the seller present. The agent audio records themselves going room by room, dictating freely and honestly everything they observe: every preparation item, every cosmetic issue, every system that needs attention, every question about service history, every vendor to identify. This recording becomes the source of truth for the ABC List, the vendor initiation list, the seller letter, and ultimately the Features List and MLS description. You cannot write the comprehensive seller letter accurately without this walkthrough first.

This is a heavy operational lift. The moment the contract is signed, the agent must simultaneously initiate conversations with a large number of people — the TC, brokerage staff, vendors, the seller, and internal team members — while setting up all of the administrative infrastructure that will carry the listing through to closing. The Solo Walkthrough / Agent Dictation Skill and Listing Prep Pro: Intake & Operations are specifically designed to orchestrate all of this at once. The checklists below exist to make sure nothing gets missed.
